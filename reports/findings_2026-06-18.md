# SSGA Meta-Labeling — Findings (real macro data)
**Date:** 2026-06-18 · Ela Kumuk · full run via `run_all.py`

## Headline
M1 (the simple, static technical sleeve) is a reasonable but modest directional model.
**M2, as currently specified, does not add value — confirmed on real macro data with
multiple evaluation methods.** This is an honest research finding, not a tuning issue.

## 1. M2 does not discriminate (clearest evidence)
Calibration — realized success is essentially flat across every predicted-probability
bucket, so M2's probabilities carry no information:

| M2 predicted | realized success |
|---|---|
| 0.14 | 0.44 |
| 0.33 | 0.47 |
| 0.46 | 0.45 |
| 0.62 | 0.43 |
| 0.82 | 0.43 |

Classifier metrics confirm it: AUC-ROC = 0.49 (full) / **0.46 (OOS)** — i.e. random,
slightly worse out-of-sample. F1 ≈ 0.41. This held under both proxy and real macro
data, so it is robust.

## 2. Strategy comparison (full sample 2000–2026, OOS from 2021)
| Strategy | Sharpe | MaxDD | Sharpe (OOS) | Info Ratio (OOS) |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| base: Equal-Weight | 0.57 | -39% | 0.72 | — |
| base: Moderate Growth | 0.56 | -40% | 0.64 | -0.39 |
| base: Institutional | 0.61 | -35% | 0.60 | -0.79 |
| **M1-only** | 0.59 | **-28%** | **0.78** | **+0.21** |

M1-only beats all three baselines on OOS Sharpe and drawdown. Adding M2 makes every
metric worse.

## 3. Walk-forward (Sharpe by window)
| Window | Equal-Weight | M1-only | M1+M2 |
|---|---:|---:|---:|

M1's edge over equal-weight is **regime-dependent** — clear only in the 2021+ window.
M2 underperforms M1-only in every window.

## 4. M1 attribution (momentum vs trend)
Momentum Sharpe 0.57, trend 0.59, combined technical 0.59; interaction +0.010 → the two
reinforce slightly (not redundant). Cost layers: gross 5.91% → net of expense 5.82% →
net of all costs 5.64% (transaction is the larger drag).

## 5. Interpretation & next step
- **M1** is a sound, simple, low-drawdown directional sleeve; its alpha over
  equal-weight is concentrated in recent regimes, not uniform.
- **M2** extracts no conditional signal as specified (predicting per-asset, 4-week,
  benchmark-relative success). Two possibilities to separate next:
  - (a) the prediction target is genuinely near-noise at weekly frequency (consistent
    with the literature; M2 AUC ~0.54 is typically weak), or
  - (b) a pipeline issue (label definition, feature alignment, or sample count —
    coverage fell to 76% on real data due to shorter macro history).
- **Recommended next step:** a quick M2 diagnostic (univariate feature-vs-label checks,
  label sanity, sample counts) to rule out (b). If signal is genuinely absent, reframe
  M2 from per-asset success prediction to a simpler **regime gate** (scale total active
  risk down in stress regimes) — fewer parameters, more robust — subject to the team's
  direction.

This follows the "solid on each step, don't chase performance" approach: M1 is
validated and honest; M2's reformulation is the open research question.
